Introduction¶

Are you a student at the University of Maryland? If so, do you live on-campus? Or are you a commuter with a dining plan?

Well, if you've said yes to those questions, then you most likely eat at the college's dining halls pretty regularly. And, at least once, you've probably walked in thinking you'll eat healthy today, but not knowing what is "healthy" or what types of food you should eat to achieve your goal, you end up getting a bunch of random junk that, is more likely than not, horrible for you. I know I have, many, many times...

Well, I've had it. And so should you!! We should be mindful of what we put into our bodies. That is why I decided to scrape UMD's nutrition facts, and using this data find the best, and worst foods for our bodies. We can also make a conclusion on the nutrition facts of Breakfast vs Lunch vs Dinner, to see what should be the 'biggest meal of the day' as a University of Maryland student.

def getLinks(URL):

    menu = requests.get(URL)
    soup = BeautifulSoup(menu.content, "html.parser")
    links = []
    
    i = 0
    for a_href in soup.find_all("a", href=True):
        if i > 8:
            links.append("https://nutrition.umd.edu/" + a_href["href"])
        i+=1
    del(i)
    
    links.pop()
    return links

First, I start off by getting a list of links to every food item on the website. That way, we can work on getting the specific nutrition facts.

    h = []
